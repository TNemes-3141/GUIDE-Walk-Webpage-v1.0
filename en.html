About the project

What is GUIDE-Walk?

The supporting infrastructure for the blind and visually impaired is constantly being expanded: floor guidance systems and traffic lights with audio signals are a matter of course for us nowadays, trained guide dogs accompany visually impaired people in their everyday lives. The new technology of artificial intelligence (AI) can help to create exciting technology with which blind people can become more independent and more easily navigate through the city on their own.

GUIDE-Walk is a digital, portable assistance system that helps blind and visually impaired people orientate themselves in traffic and improve their safety. With the use of a camera, a motion sensor and a LiDAR sensor, the device records images and information from the environment and processes them. The integrated AI learns to recognize obstacles and dangers such as cars, pedestrians, bicycles and traffic lights and warns the blind wearer via headphones.
The GUIDE-Walk 2.0 is the successor to a prototype, which was created last year as part of a project work for Jugend forscht (German Junior Science Contest).

About the creator

My name is Tamas Nemes, I am 17 years old and a high school student in Bavaria. The possibilities of AI and its technical applications have interested me for a long time. I came up with the idea of ​​developing GUIDE-Walk through some friends of mine who themselves struggle with a visual impairment and told me about the challenges they face in everyday traffic.

I'm working on the the blind guidance system for two years. During this time, I learned a lot and was able to get to know the effort involved in product development. The goal that has always motivated me was to create a safe everyday helper for the blind. Two affected people who tested the device can confirm my intention: AI can and should continue to be used to help people and perhaps give them a new perspective through the "eye of the computer".
